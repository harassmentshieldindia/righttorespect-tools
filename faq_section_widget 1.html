💬 Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about debt collection harassment

⚖️ What are my basic rights as a borrower? +

As a borrower, you have fundamental rights protected by RBI guidelines:

  • Right to dignity: No abusive language or intimidation
  • Right to privacy: No sharing of loan details with third parties
  • Right to reasonable communication: Calls only 8 AM-7 PM, max 3 per week
  • Right to fair treatment: Professional conduct from recovery agents
  • Right to complain: File complaints against harassment
Remember: You must repay your loans, but harassment is never acceptable!
📞 How many calls can recovery agents make to me? +

According to RBI guidelines:

  • Maximum 3 calls per week directly to you
  • Only between 8 AM to 7 PM on working days
  • No calls on Sundays and public holidays
  • Cannot call your family/friends repeatedly
Violation: If they call more than this, it's harassment - file a complaint!
🤬 Can recovery agents use abusive language? +

Absolutely NO! Recovery agents cannot:

  • Use foul, abusive, or threatening language
  • Shout, scream, or intimidate you
  • Make personal attacks or insults
  • Use discriminatory language
  • Threaten violence or harm
Action: If this happens, record the call (if legal) and file police complaint immediately!
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Can they call my family members? +

Limited contact is allowed, but with strict rules:

  • Only if you're unreachable for reasonable period
  • Cannot discuss loan details with family
  • Cannot harass or threaten family members
  • Cannot call repeatedly to same family member
  • Must maintain dignity and professional conduct
Tip: Always answer calls from your lender to prevent family contact!
📝 How do I file a complaint against harassment? +

Follow this step-by-step process:

  • Step 1: Document everything (calls, messages, dates)
  • Step 2: Complain to your bank's grievance officer first
  • Step 3: Call RBI Ombudsman at 14448
  • Step 4: File online at cms.rbi.org.in
  • Step 5: Police complaint if threatened (call 100)
Free Service: All RBI and police complaints are completely free!
🕐 How long does RBI take to resolve complaints? +

RBI complaint timeline:

  • Acknowledgment: Within 7 days
  • Bank's response: 30 days to bank
  • Final resolution: Usually within 60 days
  • Complex cases: May take up to 90 days
Important: Keep following up if no response within timeline!
⚖️ Is harassment a criminal offense? +

Yes! Harassment can be criminal under:

  • IPC Section 503: Criminal intimidation
  • IPC Section 506: Punishment for criminal intimidation
  • IPC Section 504: Intentional insult
  • Various state laws: Additional protections
Action: File FIR at police station for criminal harassment!
💰 Can I get compensation for harassment? +

Yes! You can get compensation through:

  • Banking Ombudsman: Up to ₹20 lakhs compensation
  • Consumer Court: Compensation for mental harassment
  • Civil Court: Damages for defamation/harassment
  • Criminal Court: Punishment for offenders
Recent Cases: Banks have paid lakhs in compensation for harassment!
🚨 What should I do if I'm threatened with violence? +

Immediate action required:

  • Call Police immediately: Dial 100
  • Don't argue or provoke the caller
  • Record the threat if possible
  • Inform family members for safety
  • File written complaint at police station
  • Contact your bank to report the incident
Emergency: Your safety comes first - don't hesitate to call police!
📱 Should I record harassment calls? +

Recording calls can be valuable evidence:

  • Check state laws: Some states require consent
  • Use call recording apps on smartphones
  • Keep original recordings safe
  • Make transcripts of important calls
  • Backup on cloud storage for safety
Legal Tip: Even if recording isn't admissible, it helps you remember exact details!
💳 Can I stop paying EMIs if harassed? +

No, you cannot stop EMIs due to harassment.

  • Harassment doesn't cancel your loan obligation
  • Stopping EMIs will worsen your credit score
  • Additional penalties and interest will apply
  • Legal action can still be taken by lender
Better Option: File harassment complaint while continuing to negotiate repayment!
🏠 Can recovery agents enter my house forcibly? +

Absolutely NOT! They cannot:

  • Enter your house without permission
  • Break locks or force entry
  • Seize property without court order
  • Stay on your property if asked to leave
  • Threaten to take your belongings
If this happens: Call police immediately (100) - this is criminal trespass!
